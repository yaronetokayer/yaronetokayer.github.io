Welcome to my interim website. I am a doctoral candidate in the Department of Physics at Yale University studying cosmology and galactic dynamics with advisor Frank van den Bosch. I also work with Priyamvada Natarajan and Michael Koss

My work uses physics computer simulations, data processing and visualization, and traditional analytical methods to better understand cosmology.

A more detailed website with my research projects and interests is coming soon. Meanwhile:

  • Recent research projects include:
    • Probing the structure of dark matter structures by modeling their growth
    • Reconciling gravitational lensing measurements with cosmological models
    • Quantifying the bias in X-ray surveys of supermassive black holes
